UK COVID Lockdown: What can the signatories tell us about this pandemic?



COVID-19 is a form of coronavirus that first appeared in Wuhan, located in the central Chinese province of Hubei, at the end of last year. The infectious respiratory disease has since been declared by the World Health Organisation (WHO) as a pandemic as it was spreading rapidly throughout the world. Today, more than a million cases have been recorded in over two hundred countries and territories. Many of these countries had to take drastic actions to shut their borders and limit free movement in the hope to fight this invisible threat.

British Prime Minister Boris Johnson confidently announced during the early phase of the country’s coronavirus epidemic in mid-March that Britain will remain open for business. Citing a defiant mayor from the film Jaws who kept a seaside town open despite shark attacks in 2006, an old quote by Johnson is suddenly relevant as he wanted to pursue ‘herd immunity’ in a stark contrast to his European counterparts. While this herd immunity argument is highly controversial and the country has since implemented far more restrictive measures in a seeming U-turn of policy, there has been a petition all along calling for such containment procedures.

With over 400,000 signatories, the petition on the UK government’s parliament website calls for the UK to “follow suit the containment procedures of countries that have been greatly affected by COVID19 such as Italy” ,“restrict unnecessary travel between towns and cities”, as well as to “spend money to contain the virus rather than wait for more causalities”. Could the signatories of this petition reveal any insight about who are the hundreds of thousands of people calling for greater restriction on movement and why have they been relatively more concerned than others?






As London seems to have the highest number of signature counts, it will be our primary area of focus. The capital city is incidentally also a coronavirus hotspot with over 40% of the country’s cases being based there. With the help of the latest census and other official data, it is possible to delve into the different constituencies in London and deduce the characteristics of the Londoners wanting a curtailment of movement.
